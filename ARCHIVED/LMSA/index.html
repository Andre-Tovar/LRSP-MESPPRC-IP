Level 1 - Foundry Morning

You're building the bus schedule for Logistical Models for Scheduling Architects (LMSA), a manufacturing firm that needs employees collected from roads around the district and brought back to the LMSA manufacturing plant. Be precise! The fate of their mornings are in your hands.

Difficulty Easy
Budget $100

Mission Brief

  • Build a fleet by purchasing buses from the bus rental.
  • Click on brown "intersection" nodes in sequence to form a route.
  • Click on blue "bus stop" nodes to pick up LMSA employees.
  • Bring passengers back to the LMSA manufacturing plant.
  • Clear the map without blowing the budget.

Mission Critical Steps

1

Choose a level

Every level has a fixed road network, employee demand placed along roads, and a benchmark budget. Start with a smaller scenario, then move to tougher maps as you get comfortable.

2

Buy buses

Use the shop to build your fleet. Smaller buses are cheaper. Larger buses carry more people. Your score depends on how well you balance purchase cost, route efficiency, and bus reuse.

3

Select a bus and build a route one node at a time

Pick a bus from the lot, then click connected intersections to create a route. Routes must start at LMSA and return to LMSA. You cannot instantly reverse over the same road.

4

Pick up employees at blue demand nodes

Demand nodes live on the edges of roads, not at the brown intersections. Click on the blue nodes along your route to pick up the employees waiting there.

5

Return to LMSA and send the bus again if needed

Returning to LMSA unloads passengers and completes the route. The same bus can then be used on another route as long as it still fits inside the level's per-bus duty allotment.

6

Repeat until all demand is delivered

Keep planning until every employee has been picked up and returned to LMSA. Then submit the full fleet solution and compare your cost against the optimal benchmark.

Core Rules

  • Routes must start and end at LMSA.
  • Route time cannot exceed the route limit.
  • Bus capacity cannot be exceeded.
  • No single bus can run for more than 1 hour total across its trips.
  • Lower total cost means a better score.

Goal

Good solutions do not just finish the level. They finish it with clean route structure, useful bus capacity, and minimal wasted travel. The goal is to minimize your total cost while still clearing the map.

Grounded Overview

About RouteCraft

RouteCraft is a route-planning game built around Logistical Models for Scheduling Architects (LMSA), a manufacturing firm that needs its employees collected from roads around the district and returned to the LMSA depot.

Each level asks you to build a complete morning pickup plan. You choose which buses to activate, draw legal routes across the fixed road network, and decide whether it is cheaper to reuse a bus or put another one into service.

Total Cost = Activated Bus Cost + Opportunity Cost

Objective Every activated bus adds its shop cost once. Every employee who is still away from LMSA adds $25 per hour until they are returned.
Tradeoff Reusing a bus saves activation cost, but waiting too long to bring employees back increases opportunity cost.
Submission A plan cannot be submitted until all demand is cleared and every route follows the rules of the level.

What You Control

  • Choose which buses to activate from the shop.
  • Build one route at a time across the fixed road network.
  • Send the same bus back out if reuse is cheaper than activating another vehicle.

What A Legal Plan Requires

  • Every employee must be picked up and returned to LMSA.
  • Every route must start and end at LMSA.
  • Bus capacity, route time, and duty time limits must all be respected.
  • The no immediate U-turn rule still applies while building routes.

What Good Play Looks Like

Strong solutions do not just clear the map. They return employees earlier, avoid wasteful detours, and reuse the right buses often enough to keep activation cost under control.

In practice, that usually means batching nearby demand, limiting empty travel, and avoiding extra vehicles unless the time savings clearly justify the added cost.
